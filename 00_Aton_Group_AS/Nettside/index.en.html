I — Philosophy
"One sun. One truth.
One obligation."

— Akhenaten, Pharaoh of Egypt, c. 1353 BC

Farao Akhenaten was history's first monotheist — he abolished the pantheon of Egyptian gods and declared the sun the singular, undivided source of all life. He was erased from history for it. Three thousand years later, we believe he was right.

Aton Group is built on the same principle: one concentrated force, directed with absolute clarity. We do not scatter capital across opportunistic bets. We identify the problems the world has learned to ignore — in any industry, at any scale — and build the companies that solve them. We are a venture builder. We take equity stakes in exchange for strategy, structure, funding and networks. Some ideas are ours. Most belong to founders who have been carrying something great for years and never took the leap. We exist for both.

V — Founder
"I did not set out to build a shipping company. I set out to solve a problem that no one else would touch."

Leif Sunde is the founder and CEO of Aton Group AS and BladeShip AS. Born in 1973 and based in Stavanger — the capital of Norwegian maritime and energy technology — he has spent three decades at the intersection of offshore operations, procurement, logistics and industrial services.

The BladeShip concept emerged from a simple observation: the wind industry had built an extraordinary machine for generating clean energy, and had given no thought whatsoever to what happens when that machine reaches the end of its life. The blades — composite fibreglass structures up to 80 metres long — cannot be recycled by any conventional method. They end up in landfill. Or worse.

Aton Group was incorporated on 28 May 2026, the same day two Norwegian patents were filed and the EIC Accelerator application was submitted to Brussels.

VI — Vision

Build something that matters.
We intend to help you do it.

By 2035, the Aton portfolio will include companies across multiple sectors — each solving a real, large, and underserved problem. BladeShip will process hundreds of thousands of tonnes of composite waste at sea. Other founders we build alongside today will lead companies we cannot yet name. The measure of success is not the number of companies — it is the size of the problems we put behind us.

Principle I
One force

We concentrate capital and intellect on structural problems — not trend-driven opportunities. If a problem is real, large, and unsolved, it deserves one company built to solve it completely.

Principle II
Founders, not managers

We do not hire people to run the companies we build — we find the founder who was born to lead it. Our job is to create the conditions for that person to succeed.

Principle III
Structure before scale

A company with a great idea and poor structure fails slowly and expensively. We invest in getting the foundation right — legal, financial, operational — before we accelerate.
