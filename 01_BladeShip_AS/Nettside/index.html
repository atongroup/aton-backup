Chapter II

The Vessel That
Is a Factory

BladeShip is not a facility. It is a vessel. A 180–220 metre special-purpose ship that sails to where the problem is — offshore at turbine hubs, or at any port with stored blades.

Rather than transporting blades to a fixed facility, BladeShip brings the factory to the blades — directly offshore at turbine hubs, or at any port worldwide. One vessel services three continents per year.

One vessel. Three continents. Twelve months. A global circular economy at sea.— BladeShip concept brief

The on-board processing line produces EN-certified glass fibre granulate (GFG) for concrete, asphalt, and cement. Future chemical recycling upgrades (CETEC, MW-solvolysis) will produce virgin-grade glass fibre worth 10× more per tonne.

I.
AHC Crane — 165t Class

PALFINGER Marine AHC crane, 6 DOF. Pneumatic pads adapt to GRP blade curvature. Offshore up to Hs=2.5m, 15 m/s wind. DP2 integrated. Lifts blades directly from turbine hubs at sea. 🛡 Patent filed 28 May 2026 · No. 20260735

II.
4-Stage GRP Processing Line

Diamond wire saw → jaw crusher → hammer mill → classification. EN-certified GFG 0.1–2mm. Concrete, asphalt, cement, GFRC. 30,000–50,000 t/vessel/yr. 🛡 Patent filed 28 May 2026 · No. 20260736

IIIa.
Mode 3a — CETEC Chemistry Industrial testbed

Stena/Vestas CETEC, industrial testbed Halmstad (multiple test batches confirmed June 2026). Separates epoxy, carbon, PET, glass to virgin grade. 10× GFG value. JEC Innovation Award 2024.

IIIb.
Mode 3b — MW Solvolysis New 2026

>95% fibre recovery at 250°C, 7–9 MJ/kg vs 22 MJ/kg pyrolysis. Compact for shipboard integration. ACS Energy & Fuels, Jan 2026.

Technology Platform

One Vessel. Then
a Global Platform.

BladeShip is not a shipping company. It is a patented technology platform — designed from day one for global replication through licensing. Three Norwegian shipyards have been contacted as potential partners for concept design and construction. The ambition is to scale through licensing once Vessel 1 proves the concept.

2026–2027
Parallel GA Design

Letters sent to Vard, Ulstein, and Green Yard Kleven in May 2026. The target is to initiate parallel concept design discussions in Q3 2026 — best design wins the Vessel 1 contract and strengthens EIC Accelerator Step 2.

2027–2028
Vessel 1 — The Pilot

One yard builds the pilot vessel. Technology validated. DNV/Lloyd's novel vessel certification — planned for TRL 6. The other two yards receive licence agreements for Vessels 2 and 3. BladeShip earns a licence fee per vessel built.

2029 → 2038
Global Replication

Asian yards (Hyundai, Samsung, HD Korea) license the design for Asia-Pacific — Taiwan, South Korea, Japan. Target: 10 vessels globally by 2038. €105M EBITDA/yr. BladeShip collects royalties on every vessel, everywhere.

The Røkke Analogy
Røkke did not build one factory trawler. He built a platform — and licensed it globally.

BladeShip follows the same logic. Patents No. 20260735 and 20260736 protect the core IP. Every vessel built by a licensed yard generates royalty revenue for BladeShip AS — independent of who operates the vessel. The licensing model transforms BladeShip from a shipping company into a global maritime IP platform with a defensible moat and recurring revenue.
